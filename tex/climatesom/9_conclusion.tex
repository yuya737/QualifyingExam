In this work, we introduce \ClimateSOM, a novel visual analysis workflow for climate ensemble datasets.
%
Past works show promise for visual analytics to unravel such complex datasets, but they do not address the three key tasks, exploration of a single set of model runs, comparison of two sets of model runs, and clustering of model runs and GCMs, that we identify in collaboration with a domain expert.
%
We leverage self-organizing maps, steerable DR, LLM-empowered query system to address these tasks, and present new insights into a precipitation ensemble dataset over two different spatial domains.
%
In addition, we solicit expert feedback from six scientists with experience studying such datasets and confirmed that \ClimateSOM can indeed discover climatologically significant insights that are novel or would require substantial manual effort to uncover otherwise.
%
We also discuss the limitations of \ClimateSOM and suggest future directions, in particular, the integration of statistical tests to quantify the significance of the insights generated by \ClimateSOM.
%
We believe that \ClimateSOM is a valuable tool for climate scientists to quickly and effectively explore and analyze climate ensemble datasets.
%
We encourage future work in incorporating novel VA techniques to address the unique challenges in climate science.
